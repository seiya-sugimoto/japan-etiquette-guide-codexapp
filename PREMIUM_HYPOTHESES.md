# Premium Hypotheses

This document organizes possible Premium directions for `Japan Etiquette Guide`.

The goal is not to decide pricing immediately.

The goal is to answer a more basic question first:

**What would make a first-time visitor feel that Premium is genuinely worth paying for?**

## Current Reality

Right now, the free layer is already strong.

- 69 total scenes exist
- core first-trip etiquette remains highly useful without payment
- trust is strong because the app does not feel manipulative

That is good for product trust.

But it also means:

- "more scenes" alone may not be a strong enough reason to pay
- deeper cultural notes alone may be interesting, but not always purchase-worthy
- Premium needs to add a clearer layer of value

## What Is Not Yet Validated

We do **not** yet know which of these users would actually pay for:

- deeper culture
- more specialized scenarios
- phrase support
- travel convenience tools

So Premium should be treated as a set of hypotheses to test, not a solved product.

## Hypothesis 1: Deep Context Premium

### Idea

Users pay for a deeper understanding of Japanese cultural context.

This means Premium would add things like:

- expanded "why this matters" sections
- local nuance and variation
- when rules are flexible vs sensitive
- background on social meaning and atmosphere

### Example content

- why ryokan etiquette feels more formal than a normal hotel
- how shrine / temple behavior differs by place and level of ceremony
- when onsen rules are strict, and when they vary
- how social situations feel different in quiet, formal, or crowded settings

### Why someone might pay

- they want to feel more confident
- they enjoy understanding culture, not just rules
- they care about avoiding awkwardness in a respectful way

### Weakness

This may be interesting without being urgent.

Many travelers may think:

"Nice to know, but not worth paying for right now."

### Likely demand

- emotional appeal: medium
- practical urgency: low to medium
- purchase strength: low to medium

### Best use

This works better as a **supporting layer**, not the only Premium value.

## Hypothesis 2: Situation Pack Premium

### Idea

Users pay for scenario packs that fit a specific trip style.

Instead of selling "more etiquette," Premium sells:

- better guidance for the kind of trip they actually have

### Example packs

#### Special Stays

- ryokan
- staying with locals
- hostels and dorm rooms
- residential etiquette

#### Social & Nightlife

- all-you-can-drink
- karaoke
- bars
- social invitations

#### Events & Crowds

- live concerts
- sports events
- theme parks
- festivals and crowd flow

#### Independent Travel

- rental car
- service areas
- regional transport edge cases

### Why someone might pay

- their trip includes these situations
- they want help in places where mistakes feel more personal
- the pack feels relevant to their actual plan

### Weakness

If a user does not plan to use that pack, value drops quickly.

So this is stronger when the app can say:

"This pack matches your trip."

### Likely demand

- emotional appeal: medium
- practical urgency: medium to high
- purchase strength: medium

### Best use

This is a strong structural layer for Premium, especially if packs are clearly named and visually grouped.

## Hypothesis 3: Convenience Premium

### Idea

Users pay not for more reading, but for a smoother trip.

This means Premium would add tools such as:

- saved guides
- trip collections
- situation checklists
- polite phrase cards
- offline bundles
- recommendations by trip type

### Example features

- "Before you go to the onsen" checklist
- "Hotel stay basics" saved collection
- phrase card:
  - "Could you please help me?"
  - "Is this area okay for luggage?"
  - "I am sorry, is this allowed here?"
- business trip essentials
- family trip essentials

### Why someone might pay

- this saves time during the trip
- it feels practical and reusable
- it reduces stress in real moments
- it feels like a travel tool, not just reading material

### Weakness

This requires more product work than just adding content.

It may involve:

- saved state
- offline strategy
- recommendation logic
- better collection UX

### Likely demand

- emotional appeal: medium
- practical urgency: high
- purchase strength: high

### Best use

This is the strongest candidate for a real paid reason, especially in a buy-once app.

## Comparison

| Hypothesis | What user pays for | Strength | Weakness | Current recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Deep Context | richer cultural understanding | elegant and brand-aligned | may feel optional | supporting value only |
| Situation Packs | trip-specific scenario bundles | easy to explain and package | only relevant to some users | strong Premium structure |
| Convenience | time-saving tools and phrase help | easiest to justify paying for | needs more product work | strongest long-term value |

## Recommended Direction

The most realistic Premium direction is:

### Primary value

**Convenience + Situation Packs**

This gives users a clearer reason to pay:

- "This makes my trip easier"
- "This fits the kind of trip I am taking"

### Secondary value

**Deep Context**

This makes Premium feel richer and more distinctive, but should not be the main purchase reason by itself.

## Suggested Premium MVP

If Premium were to become a real product, the first version should probably be:

### 1. Situation Packs

- Special Stays
- Social & Nightlife
- Events & Crowds

### 2. Light Convenience Layer

- saved collections
- phrase cards for difficult moments
- simple pre-visit checklists

### 3. Selected Deep Context

- expanded notes for shrine
- expanded notes for temple
- expanded notes for onsen
- expanded notes for ryokan

This mix is stronger than:

- charging only for deeper essays
- charging only for a few extra scenes

## What To Test First

Before real billing, the best things to test in the mock UI are:

### Test A

Does a user react better to:

- "Unlock deeper cultural notes"

or

- "Unlock travel packs for your trip"

### Test B

Does a user react better to:

- more scenes

or

- phrase cards / saved collections / checklists

### Test C

Which packs feel most compelling?

- Special Stays
- Social & Nightlife
- Events & Crowds
- Business / Long-Stay

## Practical Recommendation For This Project

For `Japan Etiquette Guide`, the best near-term move is:

1. keep the free core strong
2. keep `premium-only` limited for now
3. reshape Premium around named packs
4. design 1 small convenience layer
5. treat deep cultural context as an enhancer, not the whole product

## Next Step

The next practical step should be:

**define 3 to 4 named Premium packs and map current premium-only scenes into them**

That will make the Premium experience easier to understand, easier to present, and easier to test.
